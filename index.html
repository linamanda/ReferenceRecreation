The International Typography Style became popular with the look of many Swiss cultural institutions who depended deeply on the impact and effect of their posters as advertising material.

International

Typographic

Style

This style emerged in Switzerland in the 1950's and slowly became worldwide by the 1970's, as the name suggests the style had a strong dependency on typographic elements. Along with these elements the style also was recognizable by the use of a mathematical grid to supply the design with an overall order and unified structure. The designers used almost exclusively sans-serif fonts, strongly favoring helvetica, usually formatting the type with a flush left and regged on the right. The type would usually be blocked together and placed strategically in the design to be noticed, and the illustrations used were mostly photographs instead of, or combined with illustrations.
